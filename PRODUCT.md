# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Car enthusiasts and hobbyists who actively build, modify, and maintain their own vehicles. Used alone, at home or in the garage, on a desktop browser. The user knows their car deeply — they don't need onboarding, they need a fast log.

## Product Purpose

VroomShop is a local-first car management app. No backend, no account, no sync — everything lives in the browser. Users track builds per car: mods installed, maintenance records, parts on the wishlist, open issues, and to-dos. Success means a user can open it mid-build and log a new mod in under 10 seconds.

## Brand Personality

Enthusiast, precise, dark. The tool should feel like a gearhead built it for themselves — dense, confident, no marketing sheen. Orange accent is functional: it marks primary actions and status, not decoration.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards (blue accent, sidebar nav, wide spacing, soft shadows everywhere)
- Dark dashboard clones (same component vocabulary as every admin panel)
- AI-generated portfolio aesthetics applied to tool UI

## Design Principles

1. **The tool disappears into the task.** Users are logging a mod or checking maintenance history — the UI should never slow them down or ask for attention.
2. **Dark is native, not a preference.** This is not a light-mode app with a dark toggle. The dark palette is load-bearing.
3. **Orange marks what matters.** Accent color earns its place: primary actions, current state, alert badges. Never decoration.
4. **Dense by default.** Car data is rich. Tables, stat rows, and tag lists should be compact enough to scan, not airy enough to feel like a landing page.
5. **Local is a feature.** No spinner waiting for a server. No empty state waiting for an account. The app is always ready.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as the floor. All interactive elements keyboard-navigable. Focus indicators visible. Reduced motion respected.
